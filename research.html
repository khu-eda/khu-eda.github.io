Research Overview

The AI‑aided Design Automation (AIDA) Lab investigates next‑generation methods for automating and optimizing electronic design. Our work combines machine learning, optimization, and circuit design principles to tackle challenges in design productivity, quality, and scalability.

We focus on three core areas: (1) integrating AI into the Electronic Design Automation (EDA) flow, (2) design and technology co-optimization, and (3) developing robust physical design methodologies for advanced semiconductor nodes.

Research Themes

AI-aided Design Automation

We develop intelligent algorithms that learn from past designs data to guide decision-making in both digital/analog design. Our approaches enable faster convergence to optimized solutions with fewer design iterations.

Design and Technology Co-Optimization (DTCO)

DTCO is essential for pushing the limits of modern semiconductor design. We co-optimize standard cell architectures and physical constraints with technology-specific rules and capabilities.

Design Methodology for Advanced Nodes

As technology scales into the sub‑5nm, new technology has evolved. We build flow-compatible tools and methodologies for advanced technology.
